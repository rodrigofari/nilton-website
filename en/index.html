Coached by a competitor. Built on the island's edge.

Madeira Surf Progress is led by Nilton Freitas — competitive surfer, trained under high-level coaches, born and raised on the volcanic coastline he now coaches on. Years on the Portuguese national circuit taught him how to read waves at speed; years of technical training taught him how to teach what he learned.

The brand exists because Nilton saw a gap. Madeira is one of Europe's most serious surf destinations, but most visiting surfers spend their week guessing the forecast, surfing the wrong spot for the day, or stuck in group lessons that don't move them forward. Madeira Surf Progress is built around the opposite: the right spot for the conditions, the right wave for your level, structured technical feedback every session.

Whether you're standing up for the first time at Machico or stepping onto Jardim do Mar's point for the first serious right-hander of your life, the methodology is the same — read the day, pick the wave, refine the technique, ride forward. No shortcuts. Real progression.

  • What level do I need to be?

    Every level — beginner, intermediate, advanced. The methodology is the same regardless: read the conditions, pick the right spot for your level, work on technique that actually moves you forward. Beginners surf forgiving sand-bottom waves at Machico; intermediates and advanced surfers head to the south-coast points when conditions line up. The session is built around you, not the other way around.

  • How is this different from a regular surf school?

    A traditional surf school is built around delivering lessons at scale — usually group lessons, fixed times, beginner-focused, often at the same beach regardless of conditions. Madeira Surf Progress is built around progression. Private or near-private coaching, forecast-driven scheduling, spot selection based on the day's conditions and your specific goals. Every booking is shaped around moving you forward, not filling a class.

  • How do I know if a 1-Week or 2-Week programme is right for me?

    The 1-Week programme is the right call if you want serious, structured progression in a defined window — daily coaching, daily surfing, focused work on technique and ocean reading. The 2-Week Intensive Camp goes further: it's an application-based, performance-led programme designed for surfers who want transformation, not improvement. If you're not sure, send a message — we'll talk through your level and goals and recommend the right fit.

  • How does scheduling work?

    Sessions are forecast-driven, not fixed. Once your booking is confirmed, we lock approximate dates; the exact spot and time get confirmed 24–48 hours ahead based on the swell window. No fixed schedule means no wasted sessions — only days when there's something worth surfing.

  • Do I need to bring my own board?

    Boards and wetsuits are included with every session. If you'd rather bring your own quiver, that works too — we'll meet at the spot.

  • What about transport?

    Pick-up and drop-off from your accommodation are available across the south coast and Funchal. North-coast pickups are arranged case by case depending on conditions and where the swell is working that day.

  • What if there's no swell during my visit?

    If conditions are completely flat, we either reschedule, switch to a coastal walk and spot-knowledge tour, or refund the session. You don't pay to surf nothing.

  • Is Madeira a good destination for first-time surfers?

    For absolute beginners, yes — at Machico bay, where the bottom is sandy and the waves are forgiving. The south-coast point breaks and rock breaks elsewhere on the island are advanced-only. Madeira's reputation as a serious surf destination comes from those advanced waves, but Machico keeps it accessible for anyone learning.

  • How do I pay?

    Bank transfer or in person on the day, depending on your stay length and the programme you book. We'll agree the method when we confirm.
